How We Measure

We tried to find a universal law of how the world discovers new things. We didn't find it. We found something more interesting.

"The explored data show a broad distribution of diffusion times (L) across emerging technologies, with strong heterogeneity between domains. The biotech_medical subset presents the lowest variance (CV = 0.222), while the AI domain shows high heterogeneity (CV = 0.452). Results are limited by selection bias, small sample size, and possible censoring effects."

// three locked definitions

The Core Measurements

t_start
First public emergence
Seminal paper OR documented commercial launch OR recognized founding event. Must be justified and documented with a primary source.
t_peak
First structural consolidation
First window of ≥12 consecutive months with Google Trends ≥25/100 Worldwide. Updated from 3 to 12 months in v1.1 to distinguish structural attention from hype.
L
L = t_peak − t_start (months)
Descriptive lag metric — not a physical constant. Sensitive to t_start definition and selection bias. Represents the observable gap between emergence and sustained public attention.
Important. Zorthex is an attention mapping heuristic over selected technological narratives — not a universal law. Results are calibrated on dataset v1.2 and are not generalizable beyond the observed sample. Classifications are operational heuristics, not ontological categories.
// threshold

The 25/100 Rule

Activation Threshold — Google Trends Worldwide
≥ 25/100
Empirically set based on sensitivity testing across dataset v1.2. At this value, the separation between sustained structural signals and transient attention peaks is most stable under tested conditions (range 20–30). This is one of three attention proxies — Google Trends, Wikipedia Pageviews, Reddit — not the sole determinant of classification.

Classifications

STRUCTURAL
12+ consecutive months ≥ 25/100
Permanent classification once acquired. Public attention consolidated at structural level.
OBSERVATION
1–11 months above threshold
Signal forming. Classification may change — monitoring required.
BUBBLE
Peak without 12-month consolidation
Permanent classification. Attention spike that did not consolidate into structural phase.
Classifications observed within dataset v1.2 — operational heuristics, not universal laws. The 12-month rule is a conventional threshold, not a natural boundary. Borderline cases (9–11 months) require active monitoring.
// limitations

What This Is Not

01
Survival Bias
Only technologies that reached mainstream are included. Technologies that failed to diffuse are not represented.
02
Selection Bias
Technologies chosen for historical prominence, not random sampling. Results cannot be generalized.
03
Proxy Limitation
Google Trends measures search interest, not understanding or adoption. Biased toward English-speaking populations.
04
Small Sample
n=12 structural total. No robust statistical inference possible. Target for v2.0: n≥20 per cluster.
05
t_start Subjectivity
Identification of the founding event involves judgment. v2.0 will introduce confidence bands based on alternative t_start candidates.
06
Operational Window
L is measurable only after t_peak. In real time, historical sector L is used as a probabilistic estimate, not a certainty.
